About Me

I am interested in designing methods that can be used as brain-machine interfaces for assisting severely paralyzed patients, to make them able to better communicate with their environment. My approach consists of using state-of-art methods in deep learning, machine learning and natural language processing to design speech or motor decoding algorithms and understanding the neural bases of learning, memory, visual perception or speech in the brain. Furthermore, I am interested in developing algorithms that can help us extracting and visualizing the latent information embedded in the neural activity patterns or in the state-of-the-art neural networks, as a tool to understand how the human brain or neural networks learn and also, to step towards developing methods to bridge AI and neuroscience.
